Seven wonder of the world

Seven Wonders of the World (2000–2007) was a campaign started in 2000 to choose Wonders of the World from a selection of 200 existing monuments.

1) Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza (also known as the Pyramid of Khufu or the Pyramid of Cheops) is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex bordering present-day El Giza, Egypt. It is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and the only one to remain largely intact.

2) The great wall of china 

The Great Wall of China is the collective name of a series of fortification systems generally built across 
the historical northern borders of China to protect and consolidate territories of Chinese states and empires 
against various nomadic groups of the steppe and their polities.

3) Petra 

Petra, originally known to its inhabitants as Raqmu, is a historical and archaeological city in southern Jordan.
 Petra lies on the slope of Jabal Al-Madbah in a basin among the mountains which form the eastern flank of the Arabah
 valley that runs from the Dead Sea to the Gulf of Aqaba.

4) Colosseum 

The Colosseum or Coliseum , also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre (Latin: Amphitheatrum Flavium; Italian: Anfiteatro
 Flavio [a?fite'a?tro 'fla?vjo] or Colosseo [kolos's??o]), is an oval amphitheatre in the centre of the city of Rome, Italy.
 Built of travertine limestone, tuff (volcanic rock), and brick-faced concrete,[1] it was the largest amphitheatre ever 
built at the time and held 50,000 spectators

5) Chichen Itza 

Chichen Itza was a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people of the Terminal Classic period. The archaeological 
site is located in Tinúm Municipality, Yucatán State, Mexico

6) Machu Picchu 

Machu Picchu  is a 15th-century Inca citadel, located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru, on a 2,430-metre (7,970 ft) mountain ridge
